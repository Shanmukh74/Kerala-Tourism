Top Destinations in Kerala

Explore the beauty and culture of Kerala through these must-visit destinations.

Munnar

A hill station known for its tea plantations, misty hills, and serene atmosphere.

Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Known as the "Venice of the East," famous for its houseboats and picturesque backwaters.

Kochi (Cochin)

A port city blending modernity and tradition with its beaches, Dutch architecture, and spice markets.

Kumarakom

A popular backwater destination with bird sanctuaries and scenic views.

Thekkady

Known for the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, where you can experience boat rides and spot wildlife.

Wayanad

A beautiful district with waterfalls, caves, and sprawling spice plantations.

Varkala

A seaside town famous for its cliffs adjacent to the Arabian Sea, unique beaches, and spiritual vibe.

Thrissur

Known as the "Cultural Capital of Kerala," famous for festivals like Thrissur Pooram.

Bekal

Known for the Bekal Fort and nearby beach; the fort offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

Kollam (Quilon)

A coastal town famous for its ports and the Ashtamudi Lake.

Thiruvananthapuram

The capital city known for its historic sites, beaches, and vibrant culture.

Nelliampathy

Known for its dense forests, tea, and coffee plantations, and scenic viewpoints.

Guruvayur

A major pilgrimage center, famous for the Guruvayur Temple.

Athirappilly Waterfalls

The largest waterfall in Kerala, often referred to as the "Niagara of India."

Kovalam

A beach town with crescent-shaped beaches and vibrant nightlife.

Kolukkumalai

The highest tea plantation in the world, known for its breathtaking sunrise views.

Sabarimala

Famous for the Sabarimala Ayyappa Temple and its large annual pilgrimage.

Kozhikode (Calicut)

Famous for its beaches, historic sites, and delicious Malabar cuisine.
